# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Single user: the developer who built it. Used at home in the morning, at a desk or glancing at a phone, in the few minutes before sitting down to work. The workflow is quick: set the day type, confirm wake time, scan the schedule, check off tasks through the day. Not a tool you dwell in.

## Product Purpose

DayStack is a personal daily planning tool. You label each day of the week (Full / Half / Light) based on your commitments, set a wake time, and the app generates a concrete schedule and task checklist derived from that day type. Completion persists across devices via a Go + PostgreSQL backend.

Success looks like: opening the app in the morning, configuring the day in under 30 seconds, and having a clear schedule and checklist to return to throughout the day.

## Brand Personality

Clean, warm, grounded. Feels made by a person for themselves, not shipped by a startup. Like a well-kept notebook that also runs your day. No gamification, no badges, no streak inflation. Honest about state.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard**: white cards everywhere, blue primary buttons, Inter font, KPI metrics at the top, feels like a B2B tool.
- **Notion**: document-like UI, everything is an editable block, cluttered sidebars, very gray, feels like a blank canvas that needs configuring.
- Gamified habit trackers: rings, circle animations, streak counters as big numbers, confetti on completion.

## Design Principles

1. **Personal over generic.** This is built around specific habits (AlgoExpert, golf, protein shakes). The design should feel like it belongs to one person, not designed for a general audience of thousands.
2. **Structure that disappears.** The schedule and checklist should be effortless to scan and act on. The interface steps back after setup and lets you live your day.
3. **Morning-native speed.** Optimized for the few minutes at the start of a day. Everything should be reachable in one or two interactions. No dwelling.
4. **Warmth without decoration.** Warmth comes from spacing, type weight, and color temperature, not from illustration or decorative elements. Every warm detail earns its place through function.
5. **Honest state.** Unset is unset. Incomplete is incomplete. No encouraging copy that isn't true, no visual inflation that makes a bad day look good.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. System `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all transitions. Sufficient contrast on day-type color indicators against both surface and background. No accessibility-specific user needs known beyond the standard baseline.
